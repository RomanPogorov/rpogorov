                            15 years in design. The last two — not just a designer anymore, but a product shipper on the edge of design and code. I bring LLMs to teams that talk about AI but don't ship it, and I align engineers and designers around a single living artefact: the working prototype.
                            

        
                            Enough being static. Tired of drawing cases by hand? My flow is now code-first — Figma is the sketchbook, the prototype lives in the repo. Hypotheses get tested on a real interface; once a flow lands, I generate Figma components from the code for handoff. Same source of truth on both sides.
                            

        
                            Approach: narrow scope, brutal prioritization, then velocity. Bias toward shipping over polishing, learning over planning. Comfortable across the full stack — from systems architecture down to pixel-level UI motion.
