## Background & problem

Americor negotiates with creditors to settle users' debts for less than they owe. Negotiation takes time — settlements emerge unevenly, often months apart.

<div class="row-2">
  <PainCard tag="biz" title="Business problem">
    Engagement nosedives 2–3 months in. Onboarding cost is paid up front; revenue only lands after a settlement — early disengagement is expensive.
  </PainCard>
  <PainCard tag="user" title="User pain">
    Users start the program without clear expectations. Long quiet stretches breed confusion, frustration, and doubt — they stop believing deposits are doing anything.
  </PainCard>
</div>

## Solution breakdown

Boost confidence and engagement by making the settlement process visible, predictable, and clear.

<div class="row-2">
  <Pillar num="01" label="clarity">Cut steps in the settlement process and rewrite how it's communicated.</Pillar>
  <Pillar num="02" label="movement">Replace static creditor status with a dynamic progress signal.</Pillar>
  <Pillar num="03" label="activity">Surface what changed since last login — no need to dig through every account.</Pillar>
  <Pillar num="04" label="expectations">Set realistic timelines so users know when to expect a settlement.</Pillar>
</div>
